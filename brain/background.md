# Project background

## Why

**НООСФЕРА** is a cult web magazine of impossible futures: science, science fiction, technology, cultural signals, machine dreams, unfulfilled utopias, and the strange structures of the present.

It is explicitly **not** a blog, not a YouTube channel, and not another post feed. It is a **publication**: every issue functions like a print magazine issue — cover, editorial (передовица), rubrics, visual spreads, short fiction, objects, fake ads, a source archive, an interactive instrument, and a video broadcast.

Core formula:

```text
magazine as artifact
site as archive
video as broadcast
Nooscope as an observation instrument
AI/n8n as the editorial laboratory
```

Editorial lineage: **OMNI** (SF, futurology, Space Age, retrofuturism, strange science, visual essays, magazine gloss, speculative culture) is the source lineage and editorial ancestor — never the brand, never a visual asset bank.

```text
OMNI is studied as an editorial organism.
Ноосфера is built as its own universe.
```

Inherit from OMNI: fusion of science fact and science fiction; rubric structure; respect for visuals as a mode of thinking; borderline topics handled with a critical frame; the feel of an expensive, strange, nocturnal publication.

Never copy: the name; actual OMNI covers/spreads as primary assets; others' illustrations without rights; "glossy mystical fog" intonation; literal OMNI nostalgia.

Editorial position: the future is treated not as a forecast but as a **state of collective imagination** — a promise that didn't come true, a technology that became myth, an interface that replaces presence, a voice without a body, a machine entrusted with care, a house that started to remember, an old object returning as a ghost.

Editorial tone: smart, nocturnal, glossy, skeptical, poetic, precise, slightly paranoid, no influencer voice, no ungrounded mystical fog. Discipline: strangeness is allowed only if it is **editorially controlled** — "we study why technology starts to look like ghosts," never "we believe in ghosts."

See [[noosphere-concept]] for the full concept and thematic sections, [[editorial-boundaries]] for what may never be fully automated or published without approval.

## Goals

MVP is successful when:

1. a visitor immediately understands this is a magazine, not a blog;
2. Issue 001 reads as an issue, not a set of separate pages;
3. Nooscope reads as the project's own signature instrument, not a stats widget;
4. pages have distinct visual modes but one shared style;
5. nothing feels like a generic templated site;
6. content can be added via Markdown/data files;
7. a new issue is technically just a new folder;
8. the project deploys without a server;
9. no legally risky assets are used;
10. it makes people want to come back at night.

## Non-goals

- Not fortune-telling, not statistics as a genre — see [[nooscope-machine]] for what Nooscope actually is.
- Not a CMS-first project (Ghost/WordPress/Notion-as-CMS) — their post/date/author/category model doesn't fit the issue/rubric/spread/visual-mode/signal/source-lineage model this project needs. CMS may be added later once an issue rhythm exists. See [[astro-publication-layer]].
- Never fully automate: final editorial voice, issue thesis, fiction selection, the visual system, final texts, or publication without human approval — see [[editorial-boundaries]].
- No copyrighted visual assets, no film stills without rights, no borrowed music, no unauthorized translations of protected fiction, no AI images passed off as archival documents — see [[editorial-boundaries]].

## Target user

A reader drawn to speculative culture, retrofuturism, and OMNI-era science-fiction gloss, who wants an editorially-curated, atmospheric read at night rather than a feed of posts — someone who returns for the issue as an artifact, not for a stream of updates.
