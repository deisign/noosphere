## compiled_truth

## Definition

НООСФЕРА is a cult web magazine of impossible futures — about science, science fiction, technology, cultural signals, machine dreams, unfulfilled utopias, and the strange structures of the present.

It is a **publication**, structurally: every issue works like a print magazine issue — cover, editorial (передовица), rubrics, visual spreads, short fiction, objects, fake ads, a source archive, an interactive instrument, and a video broadcast. It is deliberately **not** a blog, **not** a YouTube channel, and **not** a generic feed of posts.

Core formula:

```text
magazine as artifact
site as archive
video as broadcast
Nooscope as an observation instrument
AI/n8n as the editorial laboratory
```

## Editorial lineage: OMNI

Reference line: **OMNI** — science fiction, futurology, Space Age, retrofuturism, strange science, visual essays, magazine gloss, speculative culture.

Rule: `OMNI is studied as an editorial organism. Ноосфера is built as its own universe.` OMNI is source lineage and editorial ancestor — never the brand, never a visual asset bank, never a literal aesthetic template.

Inherited: fusion of science fact and science fiction; rubric structure; respect for visuals as a mode of thinking; borderline/fringe topics handled with a critical frame; the feel of an expensive, strange, nocturnal publication.

Never copied: the OMNI name; actual OMNI covers/spreads as primary assets; others' illustrations without rights; "glossy mystical fog" intonation; literal OMNI nostalgia.

## Editorial position

The future is treated not as a forecast but as a **state of collective imagination**:

- a promise that didn't come true;
- a technology that became myth;
- an interface that replaces presence;
- a voice without a body;
- a machine entrusted with care;
- a house that started to remember;
- an old object returning as a ghost.

Editorial tone: smart, nocturnal, glossy, skeptical, poetic, precise, slightly paranoid — no influencer voice, no ungrounded mystical fog.

Central discipline: strangeness is allowed only when **editorially controlled**. Not "we believe in ghosts" — "we study why technology starts to look like ghosts." This same discipline governs [[nooscope-machine]] and is enforced project-wide via [[editorial-boundaries]].

## Thematic sections (rubrics)

1. **Future Archaeology** — archaeology of unfulfilled futures: smart kitchens, home robots, space-age homes, atomic living rooms, Googie architecture, automated domestic life, promises that became ruins.
2. **Machine Dreams** — machines, AI, computers, algorithms and interfaces as mirrors of human fear and desire. Not "the machine has a soul" but "humans ask the machine to play the role of memory, care, conversation, and witness."
3. **Sonic Ghosts** — voice, radio, answering machines, synthetic speech, theremin, static, recordings of the dead, AI voices, acoustic traces of presence.
4. **Antimatter / Laboratory of Impossible Things** — fringe ideas as cultural artifacts (UFO, EVP, parapsychology, strange instruments, alternative theories), handled with a critical, historico-cultural frame — never propaganda for pseudoscience.
5. **Inner Domains** — micro-worlds of objects: dust, magnetic tape, screen surface, solder, paper, plastic, scratches, circuit boards. This is the one rubric where SEM-style aesthetics belong (ordinary things become alien landscapes) — kept explicitly separate from Nooscope, which does not look at dust or microchips.
6. **Residual Media** — film, magazines, advertising, TV, interface design, and old media as machines that manufacture anticipation of the future.
7. **Night Fiction** — short fiction as a mode of observation. One issue = one main public-domain story or original fiction artifact; fiction is a full optic of the issue, not a bonus feature.
