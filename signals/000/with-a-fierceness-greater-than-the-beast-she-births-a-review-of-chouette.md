**Why now:**
The novel’s fusion of bodily horror and mythic transformation resonates with 2026’s cultural obsession with "monstrous femininity" as a metaphor for unfiltered agency—from the resurgence of *vengeful motherhood* in pop culture (e.g., *The Last of Us*’ Sarah) to the algorithmic amplification of "wild woman" archetypes in digital spaces. Oshetsky’s owl-woman hybrid forces a reckoning with the taboo of female rage as *creation*, not destruction—a counterpoint to the sanitized "empowerment" narratives of late-stage capitalism.

**Future Ghost:**
The forgotten 1970s eco-feminist idea of *women as animals as avatars of ecological vengeance*—a trope buried under second-wave feminism’s later deference to institutional power. Chouette revives it with a surreal, bodily literalness, aligning with today’s backlash against "green capitalism."
