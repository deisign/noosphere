**Why now:**
In 2026, the ghost of rock’s hypermasculine past continues to haunt digital spaces—from AI-generated "legacy artist" deepfakes to the algorithmic resurrection of dead musicians. Tsabar’s work, which dismantles the rockstar myth by literally repairing broken instruments, offers a tactile counter to these spectral imitations. Her practice aligns with a growing cultural fatigue for nostalgia-as-commodity, proposing instead a *haunted materialism*: where objects retain the residue of their past lives, and repair becomes a form of séance.

**Future Ghost:**
The idea of the *broken instrument as archive*—a physical memory bank of performances, accidents, and decay. This concept echoes forgotten 20th-century avant-garde experiments (like Nam June Paik’s "prepared pianos" or Harry Partch’s "corporeal" instruments) while anticipating a future where AI "restores" damaged artifacts, erasing their historical patina.
