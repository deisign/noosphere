**Why now:**
In an era where AI-generated imagery floods the visual field with hyperreal simulations, Koenig’s work—rooted in analog mark-making—offers a countercurrent: a meditation on the *indexical trace* as a ghostly remnant of intent. His ink patterns, neither fully present nor absent, resonate with the 2026 zeitgeist of "digital decay" (the erosion of historical media’s materiality) and the revival of pre-digital craft as a form of resistance. The show’s title’s play on "luminosities beyond the eye" prefigures the coming obsession with invisible infrastructures (quantum computing, neural lace) that demand new modes of perception.

Koenig’s work revives this idea of art as a *receptacle for absence*—a pre-modern precursor to today’s "unprompted AI hallucinations."
