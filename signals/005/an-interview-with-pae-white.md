**Why now:**
Pae White’s exhibition *Slow Winter Sun* (2023) operates as a retrofuturist séance—summoning the ghostly residues of modernist utopianism through iridescent ceramics and monochromatic paper-clay paintings. In 2026, as climate collapse accelerates, her work’s meditation on decay-as-aesthetic resonates with a culture obsessed with preserving (or mummifying) the past. The "slow winter" of the title isn’t just a season but a metaphor for cultural hysteresis: the lag between an idea’s death and its aesthetic revival.

**Future Ghost:**
The forgotten 1970s-80s movement of *biomorphic design*—where organic forms were meant to harmonize with nature—returns here as a spectral presence, stripped of its original idealism and refracted through ceramic glaze and paper clay. White’s work is a palimpsest of failed futurisms, where the "sustainable materials" of yesterday’s dreams become today’s aesthetic hauntings.
